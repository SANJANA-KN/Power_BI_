# KPI Cards and Measures in Power BI

## Introduction
KPI (Key Performance Indicators) Cards in Power BI are visual elements used to display the most important business metrics in a simple and impactful way.

They help stakeholders understand performance at a glance without deep analysis.

Measures are calculations created using DAX that power these KPIs dynamically.

---

## What is a KPI?
A KPI is a measurable value that shows how effectively a company is achieving business objectives.

### Examples:
- Total Sales
- Profit Margin
- Revenue Growth
- Number of Customers

---

## KPI Cards in Power BI
KPI Cards are visual tiles that display single important metrics.

### Example:
- Total Revenue: ₹10,00,000
- Total Orders: 5000

---

## What are Measures?
Measures are dynamic calculations created using DAX (Data Analysis Expressions).

They are not stored in the dataset but calculated at runtime.

### Example:
```DAX
Total Sales = SUM(Sales[Amount])
```

---

## Difference: Measures vs Columns

| Feature        | Measures              | Columns              |
|----------------|----------------------|---------------------|
| Calculation    | Dynamic              | Static              |
| Storage        | Not stored           | Stored in table     |
| Performance    | Optimized            | Heavy on memory     |

## Creating a KPI Card in Power BI

### Steps:
1. Go to Power BI Desktop
2. Select Card Visual
3. Drag Measure into Value field
4. Format card (color, label, font)

---

## Best Practices
- Use meaningful KPI names
- Keep metrics simple
- Avoid too many KPIs on one page
- Highlight only key business indicators

---

## Common Mistakes
- Overloading dashboards with KPIs
- Using incorrect calculations
- Not formatting properly
- Mixing unrelated metrics

---

## Key Takeaway
KPI Cards and Measures are essential for building executive-level dashboards that provide quick business insights and performance tracking.
